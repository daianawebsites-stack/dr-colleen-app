Personalized Recommendations
Boost energy & reduce fatigue
Addresses: Fatigue 4/5 · Low energy 4/5
Focus on antioxidants, omega-3 fatty acids, Vitamin D, and CoQ10 — all vital for cellular energy production. Since you're vegetarian and don't eat fish, use algae-based omega-3 supplements, fortified plant milks for Vitamin D, and flaxseeds, chia seeds, and walnuts for plant omega-3s. Soybeans and sesame seeds are good CoQ10 food sources. Your existing strength training is excellent — adding a daily 30-minute walk will amplify energy improvements further.
Stress & anxiety management
Addresses: Anxiety 3/5 · Memory & thinking goal
Hormonal changes affect serotonin activity, lowering your stress threshold. Optimizing B vitamins, quality plant-based proteins, and omega-3s (EPA+DHA) supports both mood and cognitive function. Maintain a consistent sleep schedule — you're already at a healthy 4/5 sleep quality. Relaxation practices such as 10 minutes of deep breathing daily have measurable impacts on stress resilience. Balanced diet with vitamin and mineral supplements combined with regular exercise is the most effective combination.
Mediterranean eating pattern
Supports all three health goals
The Mediterranean diet supports hormonal health, energy, and cognitive function. You're already eating whole grains daily and nuts every other day — excellent foundations. Key gaps: increase beans and legumes from once a month to 3–4 times per week, and increase fruit to 3+ portions daily. Since you have a gluten allergy, replace wheat with quinoa, brown rice, buckwheat, millet, or certified gluten-free oats. These provide equivalent fiber and nutrients. More plant-based foods will improve energy, regularity, and overall health.
Phytoestrogen & hormonal support
Addresses: Hormonal health · Hair loss
Phytoestrogen-rich foods support estrogen activity during hormonal transitions. Increase your intake of soy products (edamame, tofu, tempeh), flaxseeds, chickpeas, and lentils. These are naturally occurring plant compounds that can create mild estrogenic or anti-estrogenic effects. Flaxseeds and chia seeds also help support hormone metabolism. If you are concerned about genetic modification, choose organic or bio soy options.
Movement, strength & outdoor time
Addresses: Fatigue · Bone density · Vitamin D
Your 3–4 days of strength training is excellent — increasing lean body mass reduces the rate of aging and promotes overall health. Add a brisk daily 30–60 minute walk for cardiovascular health and improved sleep patterns. Since your routine is mostly sitting, take short movement breaks every 45–60 minutes. Walk outdoors during peak sun hours (10am–2pm) to support Vitamin D synthesis: spend 5–30 minutes with face, arms, and legs unprotected depending on your skin type and season.

Meal Plan
Breakfast Greek Yogurt Bowl with Fresh Fruit & Nuts
Ingredients
1 cup plain Greek yogurt · ½ cup mixed fresh berries · 2 tbsp mixed nuts · 1 tbsp honey (optional)
Instructions
Place yogurt in a bowl, top with berries and nuts, drizzle with honey if desired. Rich in calcium, protein, and antioxidants to kickstart the day.
Gluten-free substitute
If dairy triggers discomfort or hot flashes, replace Greek yogurt with a plant-based Greek-style yogurt (no added sugar) or 2 scrambled eggs.
Lunch Mediterranean Quinoa Salad
Ingredients
1 cup cooked quinoa · ½ cup chickpeas · ¼ cup cherry tomatoes (halved) · ½ cucumber (diced) · ¼ cup red onion (chopped) · ¼ cup Kalamata olives (sliced) · ¼ cup feta cheese (crumbled) · 2 tbsp olive oil · 1 tbsp lemon juice · salt, pepper, fresh herbs
Instructions
Combine quinoa, vegetables, olives, and feta in a bowl. Whisk olive oil, lemon juice, salt, and pepper together, then toss with the salad. Garnish with fresh herbs.
Protein & phytoestrogen boost
Add 50g bio tofu, edamame, or textured soy to increase protein and support estrogen activity. Customize vegetables to taste.
Dinner Herb-Baked Tofu with Roasted Vegetables & Broccoli
Ingredients
1 block firm tofu (pressed and sliced) · 1 cup assorted vegetables (bell peppers, zucchini, cherry tomatoes, potatoes) · 1 cup broccoli florets · 2 tbsp olive oil · 1 tsp dried oregano · lemon slices · salt and pepper
Instructions
Preheat oven to 400°F. Place tofu and vegetables on a baking sheet, drizzle with olive oil, season with oregano, salt, and pepper. Top tofu with lemon slices and bake 20–25 minutes until golden and vegetables are tender. Steam broccoli and serve alongside.
Omega-3 enhancement
Drizzle 1 tsp flaxseed oil or algae oil over the finished dish for a plant-based omega-3 boost — especially important since fish is not part of your diet. Algae sources provide EPA and DHA directly, just like fish.
